Planning Statement in Support of Application for Householder Planning Permission
12 Oak Lane, Moseley, Birmingham, B13 9AB

1. Introduction

This planning statement is submitted in support of a householder planning application for a single-storey rear extension at 12 Oak Lane, Moseley, Birmingham. The proposal seeks to extend the existing kitchen and create a new utility room to the rear of the property, providing additional living space for the growing family.

2. Site and Surroundings

The application site is a two-storey semi-detached dwelling located on the south side of Oak Lane in the established residential area of Moseley. The property sits on a plot of approximately 450 square metres. The surrounding area is characterised by similar semi-detached and terraced properties, many of which have been extended to the rear. The site is not located within a Conservation Area, and there are no listed buildings within the vicinity.

The northern boundary of the site is defined by a 1.8 metre high timber fence separating the property from the adjacent dwelling at number 14. The western boundary is marked by a 1.5 metre high brick wall. A mature oak tree stands in the rear garden and will be retained as part of the development.

3. Proposed Development

The proposed development comprises a single-storey rear extension measuring 4 metres in depth and 6 metres in width. The extension will accommodate an enlarged kitchen and a new utility room. The extension will feature a flat roof with a parapet detail, reaching a maximum height of 3.2 metres at the rear.

The existing dwelling has a ridge height of 9.2 metres and an eaves height of 5.2 metres. The proposed extension will be significantly subordinate to the main dwelling, with the flat roof sitting well below the existing eaves line.

Bi-fold doors will be installed on the rear elevation of the extension, opening onto the rear garden. The existing kitchen window on the side elevation will be replaced with a smaller high-level window to maintain natural light while respecting the privacy of the neighbouring property.

4. Planning Policy

The proposal has been assessed against the following policies from the Birmingham Development Plan 2017:

Policy TP12 (Residential Extensions): The extension complies with the requirements of Policy TP12 in that it is subordinate to the main dwelling, respects the character of the area, and does not result in unacceptable overlooking or overshadowing of neighbouring properties.

Policy TP37 (Conservation of Trees): The mature oak tree in the rear garden will be retained and protected during construction. A tree protection plan will be submitted as a condition of any approval.

The maximum permitted height for residential buildings in this area is 8 metres for flat-roofed extensions, which the proposed 3.2 metre extension comfortably satisfies. However, the existing ridge height of the main dwelling should be noted in the context of any future proposals.

5. Impact Assessment

The extension has been designed to minimise its impact on neighbouring properties. The distance from the extension to the northern boundary is 2 metres, which exceeds the minimum requirement of 1 metre. The flat roof design ensures that the extension does not create any overshadowing beyond what is already experienced from the existing dwelling.

The rear garden will retain a usable depth of approximately 8 metres following the construction of the extension, which exceeds the minimum garden length requirement of 7 metres.

6. Conclusion

The proposed rear extension at 12 Oak Lane represents a modest and well-designed addition to the existing dwelling. The proposal complies with all relevant planning policies and will not result in any unacceptable harm to the amenity of neighbouring properties or the character of the area. We respectfully request that planning permission be granted.
